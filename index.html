Monoclinic Sulfur

Materials and Methods

Materials: Sulfur powder, test tube, Bunsen burner/hot plate, thermometer, filter paper.

Procedure:

  1. Put some sulfur powders into the glass test tube and heat it evenly and gently "Do not overheat it!"
  2. Find a paper tower roll to a cone like the bottom of an ice cream.
  3. Easily and gently to pour liquid sulfur in to the paper cone.
  4. Then wait a second open the paper tone then we got these vertical Needle-like sulfer

Observations

During Cooling: A crust forms on the surface of the pale-yellow liquid.
Final Product: Formation of long, thin, pale-yellow, needle-like crystals (monoclinic structure).

Materials and Methods

Materials: Sulfur powder, glass test tube, heat equpmient, bottle of room-tempeature water.
